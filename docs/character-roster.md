# Character Roster

## Purpose

This roster defines the core cast for `Who Sent This Honourable?` during pre-production.

These characters are the current working canon for story, tone, silhouette, and gameplay planning. Specific regional language, naming detail, and voice texture can still evolve later as the world is refined.

## Cast Principles

- Every major character should be recognizable in silhouette.
- Fashion is identity, not decoration.
- Nobody should feel generic or imported from another game's archetype.
- Comedy should emerge from personality, ego, confidence, and social behavior.
- Even the funniest characters should still feel human.

## Protagonist

## Tawa "Stitch" Bello

**Role:** Playable protagonist

**Age range:** Early 20s

**Public reputation:** Fast hands, sharp tongue, always fixing something, never on time unless it matters

**Core identity:** Tailor-tech runner turned neighborhood problem-solver

### Concept

Tawa grew up around fabric, repairs, improvisation, and people who made beauty under pressure. They understand clothing as utility, status, performance, and emotional armor. When the blackout hits, Tawa becomes the connective force between districts because they can move fast, talk back, think sideways, and turn everyday materials into action.

### Personality

- stylish without trying to be adored
- impatient with fake authority
- observant
- quietly tender under the sarcasm
- practical before dramatic
- funny in a dry, cutting way

### Story Function

Tawa is the point-of-view character who links movement, fashion, neighborhood trust, and the political core of the story. They are not the strongest person in the city. They are the person stubborn enough to keep moving when everyone else is told to wait.

### Gameplay Function

- agile side-view traversal
- cable and cloth-based combat tools
- outfit-based ability switching
- social access to multiple communities

### Visual Direction

- long cropped jacket with asymmetric fabric tails
- fitted utility vest over tailored base layers
- wraps, straps, and stitched repair details
- hair silhouette that reads instantly in profile
- footwear built for speed rather than brute force

### Signature Gear

- electrified measuring tape lash
- hook spool for traversal
- reinforced shears for close-range finishers
- modular outfit pieces that alter playstyle

### Emotional Arc

Tawa begins the story annoyed but local. By the end, they become a visible symbol of collective refusal. Their growth is not about becoming a legend. It is about learning that style, skill, and anger mean more when shared.

## Core Crew

## Mama Moon

**Role:** Strategist, broadcaster, emotional anchor

**Age range:** Late 40s to early 60s

**Public reputation:** Everybody knows her voice, nobody knows all her business

### Concept

Mama Moon runs an unofficial radio operation that survives on favors, stolen electricity, old equipment, and community loyalty. She is half neighborhood intelligence network, half auntie, half political menace. The math does not matter because nobody argues with results.

### Personality

- frighteningly calm in chaos
- devastatingly funny without smiling
- affectionate in sideways ways
- impossible to intimidate
- deeply protective of local people

### Story Function

She turns scattered frustration into coordinated action. She also gives the city a voice outside official propaganda. When institutions lie, Mama Moon names what everyone can already see.

### Gameplay Function

- mission dispatcher through pirate radio
- side quest source through gossip and rumor
- unlocks district intel and public sentiment shifts

### Visual Direction

- dramatic wrappers and layered fabrics with practical pockets
- statement glasses or tinted visor
- jewelry that reads ceremonial and dangerous at once
- always looks composed even in blackout conditions

## K1 "Short Fuse" Aderemi

**Role:** Mechanic, generator expert, demolitions risk

**Age range:** Mid 20s

**Public reputation:** Can repair anything except his own decision-making

### Concept

K1 builds speaker stacks, rewires illegal backups, revives dead generators, and begins every sentence as if he has already been proven right. He is essential to the crew and also one of its greatest operational hazards.

### Personality

- brilliant
- impulsive
- loud in every sense
- charming when he should apologize
- sincere once the joke clears

### Story Function

K1 represents local engineering genius and the constant improvisation required to keep neglected infrastructure functioning. He is comic relief with real stakes because his fixes often stand between a neighborhood and total shutdown.

### Gameplay Function

- upgrades traversal and power-restoration tools
- unlocks generator and switch puzzles
- creates weapon mods with high-risk, high-style effects

### Visual Direction

- grease-stained workwear cut like custom fashion
- heavy gloves, chains, and tool belts with personal flair
- boots built for scrapyards and dance floors alike
- speaker-inspired accessories and metallic accents

## Pastor Chrome

**Role:** Community wildcard, performer, morale amplifier

**Age range:** 30s to 40s

**Public reputation:** Spiritually unpredictable, theatrically overdressed, occasionally correct

### Concept

Pastor Chrome is a preacher, host, singer, organizer, and self-appointed master of ceremony who is never far from a microphone. He sincerely believes people need hope, but he also sincerely enjoys being seen while delivering it.

### Personality

- booming
- charismatic
- vain in a survivable way
- emotionally perceptive
- unintentionally chaotic

### Story Function

He sits at the line between faith, performance, and social leadership. Pastor Chrome brings people together when despair starts thinning morale, and he provides a mirror to the minister's own performative authority.

### Gameplay Function

- morale-boosting set pieces
- crowd-control assists during public encounters
- music-linked event sequences

### Visual Direction

- chrome-trimmed robes or coats with athletic tailoring underneath
- polished shoes in completely impractical situations
- immaculate grooming and strong jewelry silhouette
- handheld mic staff or decorative cane

## Ayo Flash

**Role:** Rival courier, dancer, speed specialist

**Age range:** Early 20s

**Public reputation:** Shows off first, helps later, somehow lands on his feet anyway

### Concept

Ayo starts as an ego-driven rival who treats every emergency like an audition. Underneath the vanity is somebody who loves the city deeply and hates being treated as unserious. He becomes one of the most dynamic allies in the story once the blackout stops being abstract.

### Personality

- flashy
- competitive
- funny in a shameless way
- loyal once trust is earned
- allergic to losing an argument gracefully

### Story Function

Ayo adds youthful swagger, movement spectacle, and emotional friction. He helps keep the story lively while giving Tawa someone to bounce against who is not an elder or authority figure.

### Gameplay Function

- unlocks advanced movement routes
- supports chase sequences and co-op-style set pieces
- offers time-trial or reputation challenges

### Visual Direction

- dancer-athlete silhouette
- cropped performance jacket with reflective trim
- layered jewelry and motion-friendly trousers
- hair and accessories designed to catch movement cleanly

## Primary Antagonist

## Honourable Bemba Shine

**Role:** Main villain

**Age range:** 40s to 60s

**Public reputation:** Visionary leader, patron of progress, full-time self-advertisement

### Concept

Bemba Shine is a minister obsessed with visibility, branding, and applause. He diverts electricity from working neighborhoods to power a grotesque vanity monument and a staged public event designed to make him look historic. He does not think of himself as a thief. He thinks of himself as important enough to deserve first access to everything.

### Personality

- shameless
- eloquent in useless ways
- deeply insecure under the polish
- addicted to spectacle
- furious when mocked

### Story Function

He represents corruption as performance. He is not terrifying because he is mysterious. He is terrifying because too many people around him already know he is absurd and still keep the machine running.

### Gameplay Function

- drives citywide conflict
- appears through propaganda, announcements, and staged visits
- final boss encounter blends combat, exposure, and public collapse

### Visual Direction

- oversized statesman silhouette
- elaborate agbada- or robe-like volume interpreted through hyper-luxury political fashion
- too much gold, polished shoes, and personal insignia
- cane, sash, pins, or branded accessories featuring his own iconography

### Villain Rule

He should always be visually impressive and morally embarrassing at the same time.

## Supporting Cast

## Auntie Sewa

**Role:** Food stall owner, healer, rumor hub

**Concept**

Auntie Sewa runs one of the neighborhood's most trusted food spots. People come for the food, stay for the information, and leave with instructions whether they asked for them or not.

### Function

- food-based healing and buffs
- local memory and practical wisdom
- side quests rooted in neighborhood needs

## Inspector Sani Kole

**Role:** Mid-level state enforcer under pressure

**Concept**

Inspector Kole is not fully loyal to the Honourable, but he is trapped inside the bureaucracy that protects him. He can become obstacle, uneasy ally, or witness depending on how the story develops.

### Function

- shows the machinery around corruption
- adds moral complexity beyond simple good-versus-evil
- supports missions involving checkpoints, permits, and public order theater

## DJ Maraba

**Role:** Sound-system legend, district culture keeper

**Concept**

DJ Maraba treats speakers like sacred instruments. The blackout is personal because it silences not just music, but memory and identity.

### Function

- music-centric quest giver
- district atmosphere anchor
- boss-event setup support

## Bisi and Tunde

**Role:** School-age neighborhood runners

**Concept**

These children are too smart, too observant, and far too comfortable in places adults overlook. They spread rumors, carry messages, and understand the city's social map better than most officials.

### Function

- comic timing without becoming mascots
- alternate routes and hidden objective hooks
- grounded view of what blackout pressure does to ordinary families

## Ensemble Rules

- The cast should feel like they belong to overlapping community networks.
- Every important character should have a point of view on style, power, and respectability.
- Side characters should not exist only to praise the protagonist.
- Elders, peers, workers, artists, and children should all help define the city's personality.
- Villain-adjacent characters should reveal how corruption survives through routine, not just malice.

## Relationship Map

- Tawa and Mama Moon: trust, friction, mutual respect
- Tawa and K1: practical teamwork, constant argument
- Tawa and Pastor Chrome: skeptical affection
- Tawa and Ayo Flash: rivalry turned solidarity
- Mama Moon and Bemba Shine: ideological enemies with a history of public hostility
- K1 and Auntie Sewa: she feeds him, insults him, and keeps him alive
- Ayo Flash and DJ Maraba: ambition versus legacy
- Inspector Kole and the crew: tension built on reluctant recognition

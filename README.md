# Who Sent This Honourable?

A stylish 2D African action-comedy about stopping a vain minister who stole the city’s power for his own glory.

## Premise

Tonight is supposed to be the biggest festival in the city.

Then the power goes out.

Not because of a storm. Not because of a breakdown. Not because “these things happen.”

A shameless, image-obsessed minister has diverted the city’s electricity to power his own vanity project ahead of a public event. While the rich side of town glows, whole neighborhoods are left in darkness.

So the people decide: enough.

`Who Sent This Honourable?` is a side-view 2D action game set in a fictional Afro-metropolis full of music, noise, fashion, food, gossip, ceremony, street genius, and public absurdity. You play through districts of the city, restore light and sound, help local communities, expose corruption, and embarrass one very important man in front of everybody.

## What This Game Is

This game is about:

- style
- community
- public chaos
- humor with love, not contempt
- African city life with swagger, honesty, and soul
- fashion, music, food, and neighborhood identity
- absurd authority being challenged by ordinary people

This is not “Africa: The Game.”
It is a specific fictional city inspired by the energy, rhythm, humor, beauty, and contradictions of African life.

## Tone

The tone is funny, warm, dramatic, stylish, and honest.

The humor should feel like:

- neighborhood roasting
- TV interview madness
- fake-polite public outrage
- auntie commentary
- loud confidence
- dead-serious delivery of ridiculous things
- people surviving nonsense with style

The goal is to laugh with us, not at us.

## Core Pillars

### 1. Stylish 2D Action
Movement, combat, and traversal should feel expressive, readable, and cool.

### 2. African Fashion As Identity
Character design is a major feature, not decoration. Silhouette, fabric, hair, accessories, and color language matter.

### 3. City As Character
Each district should feel alive through music, food, slang, architecture, public behavior, and local problems.

### 4. Humor With Soul
The game should be funny because people are bold, human, dramatic, and inventive, not because they are reduced to stereotypes.

### 5. Community Over Individual Glory
This is not a lone-wolf power fantasy. The player’s story is tied to neighborhoods, crews, families, vendors, mechanics, artists, radio hosts, and ordinary people.

## Story Setup

A vain government minister has redirected the city’s power to fuel a ridiculous personal monument and public spectacle.

The result:

- blacked-out districts
- ruined festival plans
- angry residents
- broken sound systems
- spoiled food
- political excuses everywhere

The player and their neighborhood crew fight back district by district, restoring power, sound, morale, and dignity while building toward one final public humiliation for the so-called Honourable.

## Setting

A fictional African city with:

- dense markets
- minibuses and roadside chaos
- music blasting from shops and compounds
- fashion that feels ceremonial, modern, local, and iconic
- neighborhood politics
- radio chatter
- food stalls as social hubs
- nightlife, generators, banners, and public announcements
- visible inequality between districts

## Visual Direction

The visual style should feel:

- bold
- colorful
- highly designed
- silhouette-first
- fashion-led
- warm and loud

Influence areas:

- contemporary African fashion
- streetwear
- tailoring
- performance costumes
- market textures
- music video confidence
- public ceremony
- political spectacle

## Music Direction

Music is central to the identity of the game.

The soundtrack should draw from the energy of African urban sound without flattening it into one generic style. Rhythm, percussion, chanting, radio texture, crowd noise, generators, buses, vendors, and local performance culture should shape the audio world.

## Food Direction

Food is part of the soul of the city, not background dressing.

Food can appear through:

- stalls
- healing and buff systems
- side quests
- neighborhood identity
- dialogue
- visual storytelling

## Project Goals

This project aims to build:

- a stylish and funny 2D game with a strong cultural voice
- a fictional world rooted in lived feeling, not shallow aesthetic borrowing
- memorable characters with strong silhouettes and personality
- a city that feels loved, complicated, and alive
- a game that is emotionally honest under the comedy

## Development Setup

Current bootstrap:

- `Godot 4` project scaffold in `game/`
- typed `GDScript` starter scene in `game/scenes/main.tscn`
- editor defaults in `.editorconfig`
- Godot cache/export files ignored in `.gitignore`

To run once `Godot 4` is installed:

1. Open the `game/` folder as a project in Godot.
2. Run the main scene at `res://scenes/main.tscn`.
3. Use the bootstrap scene as the starting point for the first playable prototype.
